Dr. Maurice Hilleman

The Father of Modern Vaccines

Here's a time line of Dr. Hilleman's life:

  • 1919 - Born in Miles City, Montana
  • 1937 - Graduates highschool and receives a scholarship to Montana State College.
  • 1941 - Graduates from Montana State with a degree in Microbiology and Chemistry.
  • 1944 - Receives his Ph.D. in Microbiology from University of Chicago.
  • 1944 - Joins Bristol-Myers Squibb.
  • 1948 - Begins Work at the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research.
  • 1957 - Leads development of vaccine for the Asian flu pandemic.
  • 1957 - Joins Merck & Co. to oversee vaccine research and development.
  • 1963 - Loses his first wife, Thelma.
  • 1964 - Marries Lorraine Witmer.
  • 1965 - Daughter, Kirsten, is born.
  • Later Life - Develops vaccines for mumps, measles, rubella, hepatitis B, and meningitis.
  • 1984 - Retires from Merck as senior vice president, but continues work at the newly created Merck Institute for Vaccinology.
  • 2005 - Passed away in Philadelphia at the age of 85.

"Since Pasteur, he's done more for preventive medicine than anyone else."

-- Dale C. Smith
